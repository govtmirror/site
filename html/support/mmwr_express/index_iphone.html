This mobile prototype application, MMWR Express, provides fast access to the blue summary boxes in MMWR's Weekly Report.

The Morbidity and Mortality Weekly Report (MMWR) series is prepared by the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC). Often called "the voice of CDC," the MMWR series is the agency's primary vehicle for scientific publication of timely, reliable, authoritative, accurate, objective, and useful public health information and recommendations.

Summaries are viewable by specific MMWR article, or by searching for a specific subject (e.g., salmonella).

This application is a result of a collaboration between CDC's MMWR staff and the Informatics Innovation Unit staff within the Office of Public Health Scientific Services.

For content-related questions, please visit the CDC site: www.cdc.gov/mmwr

For questions regarding the application (design, new features, etc.), please contact the staff at InformaticsLab@cdc.gov.

For more information on the Informatics R&D Lab: www.phiresearchlab.org

Q: How is the "Articles" tab different from the "Search" tab?
A: The "Articles" tab allows access to an article's blue summary box information by specific MMWR article. Articles are grouped by MMWR issue, and issues are listed in sequential order (most recent publication date first). The "Search" tab allows users to search for blue summary box information via keywords related to a subject (e.g., influenza).

Q: What if I want to view the full article, not just the blue summary box information?
A: Navigate to the list of articles (either via the "Articles" tab, or by keyword via the "Search" tab). There will be a "More Info" icon (i) next to each article. Tap it, and a popover (on the iPad) or a screen (on the iPhone) will appear. Tap the "View Full Article" button on the popover/screen. It will present the full article text.

Q: How do I get the most recent MMWR issue(s) and subject information?
A: On the "Articles" tab, navigate to the list of articles. Pull down (with your finger) to refresh the content. You will see a spinning graphic while the new content is loading.
